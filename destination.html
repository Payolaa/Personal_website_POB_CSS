Machu Picchu (Perú)

Location: Country: Peru, State (Region): Cusco, Province: Urubamba
Population: The capital (Peru) has about 34 million people.
Latitud and Longitud: Approximately 13.1631° S, 72.5450° W
Small fun fact: Modern engineers estimate that 60 percent of Machu Picchu is an underground construction site. We can only see the remaining 40 percent. This doesn’t mean tunnels. The underground structures are the drainage system and the building’s foundations on such a steep mountaintop.

Alaska (USA)

Location: Country: USA
Population: Estimated to be around 740,000 people.
Latitud and Longitud: Approximately 64.2008° N, 149.4937° W
Small fun fact: The aurora borealis (northern lights) can be seen an average of 243 days a year in Fairbanks. The northern lights are produced by charged electrons and protons striking the earth’s upper atmosphere.

Cráter Azul (Guatemala)

Location: Country: Guatemala, Department: Petén, Municipality: San Benito (nearest municipality)
Population: Crater Azul is a natural site, so it does not have a resident population. The nearest town is San Benito, which has a population of about 20,000 people
Latitud and Longitud: Approximately 16.9798° N, 89.8611° W
Small fun fact: There are more than 30 volcanoes in Guatemala. Of these, the most noteworthy volcano is Tajumulco. It’s the highest peak in Central America at a whopping 4,202 metres.

Parthenon (Greece)

Location: Country: Greece, Region: Attica, City: Athens
Population: The Parthenon is located in Athens, which has a population of about 3 million people.
Latitud and Longitud: Approximately 37.9715° N, 23.7267° E
Small fun fact:The Turks, who controlled Greece in the early 19th century, stole pieces of ancient Greek sculpture from the Parthenon and sold them to the Englishman Lord Elgin. Greece has asked for the sculptures to be returned, but despite being aware of these Parthenon facts, the British Museum has so far refused.
